About

A small team. A serious commitment to your child’s progress.

Tutors Elevate started with a simple frustration: too many tuition agencies treated students like a number, and too many private tutors lacked the structure parents really needed. We set out to build something different — a small, specialist team where every tutor knows their subject inside out, every lesson is properly planned, and every parent knows exactly how their child is doing.

1

Real understanding beats memorising tricks.

We teach the why behind every method. Students who understand it remember it — under exam pressure, the ones who only memorised it forget it.

2

Patience is the most underrated skill in teaching.

Confidence is built one small win at a time. Our tutors take the time to make sure your child actually gets it — not just nods along.

3

Honesty serves students better than flattery.

We tell parents what’s really happening — what the student is strong at, what’s still wobbly, and what we’re doing about it. No glossy reports.

4

Steady growth wins.

We’re not interested in cramming. We’re interested in your child being more confident in maths in six weeks than they were today, and the same six weeks after that.

How we choose — and grow — our tutors.

Every Tutors Elevate tutor goes through an intensive in-house training programme before they take their first lesson with a student. Each has years of teaching experience and personally achieved top grades in the subjects they teach — we don’t recruit anyone who hasn’t been there themselves. Once on the team they keep developing: regular peer review of lesson plans, shared subject CPD, and direct feedback from the students they teach.

Computer Science

Computer Science is half logic, half coding — and most students are confident in one half but not the other. We bridge the gap. Our tutors have studied CS at university level and have written real production code, so they teach the subject the way it actually works, not just how the textbook describes it.

  • GCSE: Computational thinking, Python, data representation, networks.
  • A-Level: Algorithms & data structures, OOP, theory of computation, SQL.
  • NEA mentoring — the dreaded coursework, we’ve helped many through it.
  • University CS prep — recursion, complexity, software engineering.

Pick One-to-One if…

  • They have specific weak topics that need targeted work.
  • They’re aiming for top grades or competitive university courses.
  • They’ve struggled in classroom settings before.
  • They have exam-related anxiety.
  • You want maximum flexibility on schedule and content.

Pick Group Premium if…

  • They learn well alongside peers.
  • They’re confident with the basics and want structured progression.
  • They benefit from seeing how others approach problems.
  • You want strong tuition at a more accessible price point.

Can I switch? Yes — many students start in groups and move to one-to-one in the run-up to exams, or vice versa. We can review fit at any point.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Getting started

How do I book a tutor?

Start with a free 15-minute consultation. We’ll match your child to a specialist tutor and arrange a free trial lesson within a week.

Is the trial lesson really free?

Yes — no card details, no catch. If the fit isn’t right, you can try a different tutor or walk away.

How quickly can lessons start?

Usually within 7 days of your consultation. Sometimes sooner.

Lessons and tutors

Will my child have the same tutor every week?

Yes. Consistency matters — your child has their tutor, and the tutor builds a long-term picture of how they learn.

What if my child doesn’t get on with the tutor?

Tell us, and we’ll match them with a different one. No charge, no awkwardness — fit matters more than schedule.

How long are lessons?

Standard lessons are 60 minutes. We also offer 45-minute lessons for younger students and 90-minute sessions for A-Level intensive prep. Group sessions run a minimum of 1 hour 20 minutes.

What technology do I need?

A laptop or iPad and a stable internet connection — that’s it. Lessons run on Google Meet, so you just click the link we send you to join. A quiet space helps, and a second screen or paper for working through problems is handy but not required.

Subjects and exam boards

Which exam boards do you cover?

AQA, Edexcel, OCR and OCR MEI for Maths. AQA, Edexcel, OCR Gateway and OCR Twenty First Century for Sciences. AQA and OCR for Computer Science. If your child’s board isn’t listed, ask us — we may still cover it.

Do you teach for Oxbridge admissions tests (STEP, MAT, ESAT)?

Yes — Maths admissions tests are a specialism. Discuss it in your free consultation.

Do you tutor for 11+ or independent school entrance?

Currently no — we focus on Year 5 onwards in mainstream curriculum.

Pricing and payment

How does pricing work?

You pay weekly by bank transfer. Block bookings (6 or 12 lessons) come with a small discount. Full pricing is on our Pricing section.

Do you offer discounts for siblings?

Yes — please ask in your consultation. We also discount block bookings.

What’s your cancellation policy?

24 hours’ notice and the lesson is rescheduled or refunded. Less than 24 hours and the lesson is charged in full. Group no-shows are not charged.

Progress and reporting

How will I know if my child is making progress?

You’ll get a brief written progress update every 4–6 weeks, and you’re welcome to email us between updates. Close to exams, we increase reporting to fortnightly.

Will you talk to me as a parent, or only to my child?

Both — and we adapt based on age. Younger students: parents are central. Older students: we deal directly with them, with parents looped in on progress.
